Essay 2: Global economics and artistic implications of the web technologies and design techniques used :

The widespread adoption of web technologies has significantly impacted global economics and artistic expression. This essay examines the intersection of user-centric design, accessibility, and data visualization interactivity in my 'Hacking' website, exploring how these elements foster engaging learning experiences. Through a critical analysis of the site's UI/UX design and content curation, I will reflect on the challenges and successes of the project, assessing its alignment with international web development best practices. Ultimately, this research aims to demonstrate the potential of web technologies to promote inclusive, interactive, and informative digital experiences, ones that prioritizes design driven by a user-centred approach that caters to diverse needs and preferences.

The Impact of Web Technologies on Global Economics and Artistic Expression:

Web technologies have had a huge influence on global economies, specifically with regards to how they’ve provided a platform for users around the world to gain insight into the world around them. Specifically, this has been through allowing users to access information, connect with others, and express themselves creatively. With over 4.4 billion internet users worldwide (ITU, 2022), web technologies have enabled Global connectivity and community building through social media platforms (e.g., Facebook, Twitter, Instagram etc.). Furthermore, they have also allowed access to educational resources and online courses (e.g., Coursera, edX) as well as provide opportunities for digital entrepreneurship and creative expression (e.g., Etsy, YouTube). Moreover, the growth of the gig economy, with 57 million freelancers in the US alone (Upwork, 2022) demonstrates the extensive impact of the opportunities that web technologies have created for users to express themselves creatively in the digital space, leveraging the tools available to them like the technologies to increase their global audiences. By empowering users to showcase their talents and creativity, web technologies have democratized access to artistic expression and facilitated the emergence of new forms of digital art, music, and literature.

User-Centric Design: Principles and Importance:

User-Centric Design (UCD) refers to the thorough understanding of users’ behaviours, needs and preferences by conducting extensive research on the and then applying all that research to guide the design process of a certain product (Webflow, 2024). UCD takes a holistic approach to design, trying to cater as possible to users’ needs and preferences by also consulting end users during the design process to better understand their needs and their preferences. The main principles of UCD include “Data-driven decision making” as well as “Empathy”. Data-driven decision making is about acknowledging the difference in your users’ expectations and preferences and thus heavily relying on website analytic tools such as “A/B testing” to identify “pain-points” in your users’ preferences that might be evident in your site and using that information to refine your websites experience. Pain points refer to areas of design that users do not fully get as much as they may have made sense from the perspective of the designer. Furthermore , Empathy on the other hand has to do with reflecting an in-depth understanding of your audiences’ needs and preferences by incorporating throughout the design process. This principle also considers how the designers design interfaces that are intuitive enough to let the user take charge without hindering their user experience of the overall site. Not only does the latter principle ground your design within prioritizing user needs, it also ensures that your overall website fit the needs of your users as far as usability and accessibility are concerned. An example that best reflects a user centred design approach is adherence to accessibility practices such as using highly descriptive “alt” texts for the images used in your site. Demonstration of User Centred Design within my Hacking site to create meaning and successfully engage users:

With a particular prioritization of accessibility and fostering an intuitive web experience for users of different forms of preferences, I tried to adhere to some of the principles I have mentioned above. For example, to best encapsulate the experience of traversing a hacking themed site, I researched (see Theory section) the various UI design preferences that best render the experience of what a hacking site would look and feel like. The use of dark colours such as charcoal/smoky black (#141111) as well the use of warm shade of white (#FFFF0) helped in delivering the “dark side” of the internet in a visually appealing way. All the images used in the site contain a well descriptive fig captions and an alt text to allow screen readers to have the same experience of what other users are seeing. With a particular adherence to the aesthetic of simulating a hacking experience, I also relied heavily on the use of active engagement to facilitate learning and thus overall understanding. Thus, in my “Home” section of the site, I prompted users to I put their nickname to welcome them into the site. See figure 2 below. I intentionally combined this with what I envisioned would create an impression and thus set up the stage for the rest of my site; the feel of being inside a hacking terminal. Through research done on how users inclined to hacking website would expect the experience of being part of the hacking site would be, I ensured that I prioritize the aesthetic of being in a hacking site by introducing it quite early in the site. By using the “hacking terminal as well as the “green” color to align with the aesthetic and the display of the “Get started” button, I tried ensure that there is a clear visual hierarchy. This was through paying attention to the white space in between the text as well as the focal points in within the homepage which focus the attention on the centre of the screen.

In the same vein , in my “Hack” section of the website, I prompted users to engage making them click a specific link to enhance the overall understanding of what and how the cyber-attack of phishing occurs. The design choice in the simulation worked well because it mimics real-life phishing scenarios while maintaining a safe, educational environment. To achieve the latter , I tried to ensure that the experience of being in a hacking themed site aligns with what the expectations of being in a hacking site would be. I designed with the users in mind , taking into consideration the standard set for both users that are fully immersed into the intricacies of hacking and those that are not. By paying particular attention to the small details to make the overall experience of traversing the site impactful, I relied on the simple but effective usage of color contrast as well intentional colour choice selection to deliver the feel and experience at its optimal best for all users, irrespective of the knowledge they may have or not have prior to interacting with the site.
