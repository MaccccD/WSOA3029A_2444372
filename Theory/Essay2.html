Global economics and artistic implications of the web technologies and design techniques used :

The widespread adoption of web technologies has significantly impacted global economics and artistic expression. This essay examines the intersection of user-centric design, accessibility, and data visualization interactivity in my 'Hacking' website, exploring how these elements foster engaging learning experiences. Through a critical analysis of the site's UI/UX design and content curation, I will reflect on the challenges and successes of the project, assessing its alignment with international web development best practices. Ultimately, this research aims to demonstrate the potential of web technologies to promote inclusive, interactive, and informative digital experiences, ones that prioritizes design driven by a user-centred approach that caters to diverse needs and preferences.

The Impact of Web Technologies on Global Economics and Artistic Expression:

Web technologies have had a huge influence on global economies, specifically with regards to how they’ve provided a platform for users around the world to gain insight into the world around them. Specifically, this has been through allowing users to access information, connect with others, and express themselves creatively. With over 4.4 billion internet users worldwide (Morgan, 2019), web technologies have enabled Global connectivity and community building through social media platforms (e.g., Facebook, Twitter, Instagram etc.). Furthermore, they have also allowed access to educational resources and online courses (e.g., Coursera, edX) as well as provide opportunities for digital entrepreneurship and creative expression (e.g., Etsy, YouTube). Moreover, the growth of the gig economy, with 57 million freelancers in the US alone (Team , 2023) demonstrates the extensive impact of the opportunities that web technologies have created for users to express themselves creatively in the digital space, leveraging the tools available to them like the technologies to increase their global audiences. By empowering users to showcase their talents and creativity, web technologies have democratized access to artistic expression and facilitated the emergence of new forms of digital art, music, and literature.

User-Centric Design: Principles and Importance:

User-Centric Design (UCD) refers to the thorough understanding of users’ behaviours, needs and preferences by conducting extensive research on the and then applying all that research to guide the design process of a certain product (Webflow, 2023). UCD takes a holistic approach to design, trying to cater as possible to users’ needs and preferences by also consulting end users during the design process to better understand their needs and their preferences. The main principles of UCD include “Data-driven decision making” as well as “Empathy”(Webflow, 2023).Data-driven decision making is about acknowledging the difference in your users’ expectations and preferences and thus heavily relying on website analytic tools such as “A/B testing” to identify “pain-points” in your users’ preferences that might be evident in your site and using that information to refine your websites experience(Webflow, 2023). Pain points refer to areas of design that users do not fully get as much as they may have made sense from the perspective of the designer. Furthermore , Empathy on the other hand has to do with reflecting an in-depth understanding of your audiences’ needs and preferences by incorporating throughout the design process.(Webflow, 2023) This principle also considers how the designers design interfaces that are intuitive enough to let the user take charge without hindering their user experience of the overall site. Not only does the latter principle ground your design within prioritizing user needs, it also ensures that your overall website fit the needs of your users as far as usability and accessibility are concerned. An example that best reflects a user centred design approach is adherence to accessibility practices such as using highly descriptive “alt” texts for the images used in your site. Demonstration of User Centred Design within my Hacking site to create meaning and successfully engage users:

With a particular prioritization of accessibility and fostering an intuitive web experience for users of different forms of preferences, I tried to adhere to some of the principles I have mentioned above. For example, to best encapsulate the experience of traversing a hacking themed site, I researched (see Theory section) the various UI design preferences that best render the experience of what a hacking site would look and feel like. The use of dark colours such as charcoal/smoky black (#141111) as well the use of warm shade of white (#FFFF0) helped in delivering the “dark side” of the internet in a visually appealing way. All the images used in the site contain a well descriptive fig captions and an alt text to allow screen readers to have the same experience of what other users are seeing. With a particular adherence to the aesthetic of simulating a hacking experience, I also relied heavily on the use of active engagement to facilitate learning and thus overall understanding. Thus, in my “Home” section of the site, I prompted users to I put their nickname to welcome them into the site. See figure 2 below. I intentionally combined this with what I envisioned would create an impression and thus set up the stage for the rest of my site; the feel of being inside a hacking terminal. Through research done on how users inclined to hacking website would expect the experience of being part of the hacking site would be, I ensured that I prioritize the aesthetic of being in a hacking site by introducing it quite early in the site. By using the “hacking terminal as well as the “green” color to align with the aesthetic and the display of the “Get started” button, I tried ensure that there is a clear visual hierarchy. This was through paying attention to the white space in between the text as well as the focal points in within the homepage which focus the attention on the centre of the screen.

In the same vein , in my “Hack” section of the website, I prompted users to engage making them click a specific link to enhance the overall understanding of what and how the cyber-attack of phishing occurs. The most pressing challenges faced during development of this project and how they were tackled

A key challenge of executing this project was not only demonstrating an understanding of code but also the ability to showcase an in-depth appreciation of design complexity. The most difficult part was striking a good balance between the two major components. Reflecting on my Assignment 3 feedback, I recognised a lot of shortcomings from the design aspect of my project because I was focused a lot on the application of code while overlooking some important design aspects. While the feedback on the code understanding was well-received, I was disappointed on the shortcomings I had faced in the design aspect of my project. After Assignment 3’s feedback, I’ve made a conscious effort to adjust my approach in trying to find a good balance I mostly struggled with. Each day became an opportunity work not only on the code but also a day to also think extensively about the intentionality behind every design aspect of the project and how it is going to look like from the perspective of someone else. For the most part of the design aspect and the changes thereof, I relied heavily on the aspects that Imaan constructively critiqued about the design while also taking appreciation for the ones she commended. This amongst many was sticking to the convention of certain UI interfaces such as the use of scroll to top buttons, having fixed navigation at the top as opposed to having fixed footer, creating line spacing between the lines of my paragraphs, adding padding and margins to ensure that everything does not feel cluttered.Centering the text and preventing it from spanning across the page also improved readability. For the most part of my code, I followed Andre’s suggestion to align my work with modern ECMAScript 2023 standard using an async function for fetching of my API data as well as the consistency within the naming of my classes and ids.This included aligning named functions with arrow functions for clarity and consistency. Overall , while the development of this project was a huge learning curve on my end , its mostly the moments that weren’t so great in both components that I struggled to find a good balance of that helped me to improve and aim for a better outcome.

How the project aligns with professional practices in international web/app development

This project may align well with professional practices in international web/app development because it demonstrates a thoughtful integration of User-Centered Design (UCD) principles, accessible design and iterative improvement based on feedback. The following is how :
i. User-Centered Design (UCD):
The precision in the integration of User-Centered Design (UCD) , prioritizing end-user experience and showing attention to accessibility and inclusivity demonstrates a thoughtful approach towards designing with empathy and a deep understanding of meeting end-users expectations. For instance, including alt text and fig captions for images supports users with visual impairments, aligning with accessibility standards like WCAG (Web Content Accessibility Guidelines). The application of the latter instance showcases an understanding of research done to tailor the experience of the site to meet both the beginner and advanced user expectations of a hacking themed site.
ii. Intentional Design Choices and Visual Hierarchy:
Professional development emphasizes visual hierarchy to guide users through content naturally. By prioritizing aspects like color contrast, white space, and hierarchy between my tags, I ensured that users intuitively understand the content flow. By establishing a clear aesthetic from the outset and designing with intentional color schemes and typography, I created a cohesive user experience that mirrors professional practices of UI/UX designs.
iii. Attention to Detail and Interaction Design:
Designing interactive features like a clickable phishing simulation and responsive data visualization shows a strong understanding of engagement strategies. Creating an immersive experience that encourages users to interact with the content reflects the UX design principle of making users feel part of the experience, enhancing learning and retention.
iv. Realism in Design and Functionality:
By simulating real world scenarios such as phishing, I applied a valuable UX technique that professional developers use to help users understand complex topics through familiar , relatable interactions. This also indicates a strong grasp of designing with purpose, where each element serves an educational or functional purpose.

Conclusion

In conclusion , this essay has underscored the transformative potential of web technologies to create immersive , educational and user-centered experiences. Through reflecting on my hacking site’s application of user-centric design principles such as accessibility, empathy, and data driven insights, I aimed to build a site that not only reflects the aesthetic and thematic elements of hacking but also prioritizes an inclusive and engaging user experience.
