Hacking Attack: Phishing

Phishing is one of the luring techniques used by phishing artist in the intention of exploiting the personal details of unsuspected users. A Phishing website is a mock website that looks similar in appearance but different in destination. The unsuspected users post their data thinking that these websites come from trusted financial institutions. Several antiphishing techniques emerge continuously but phishers come with new technique by breaking all the antiphishing mechanisms. Hence there is a need for efficient mechanism for the prediction of phishing website (Chamanth mvs, 2018).

How Phishing works : Phishing attacks involve a four-stage process. Firstly, attackers gather information about targets through research, using social media profiling, public data, network scanning, previous data breaches, and social engineering tactics to identify vulnerable individuals or organizations. Next, they create fake emails, messages, or websites (the "bait") designed to mimic legitimate communication, exploiting human psychology and creating a sense of urgency or curiosity. This can be noticed from the tooltip in the visualization below, where the url domain name or host name is made to come to across as safe and secured , when it actually isn't. When victims interact with the bait (the "hook"), they unwittingly install malware, provide sensitive information, or initiate financial transactions (Basmah, 2024).
